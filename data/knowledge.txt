Phase 1 clinical trials focus on safety and dosage in a small group of healthy volunteers or patients.
Phase 2 clinical trials evaluate efficacy and side effects in a larger group of patients with the disease.
Phase 3 clinical trials confirm effectiveness, monitor side effects, and compare to standard treatments in large patient populations.

The FDA (Food and Drug Administration) is the regulatory body in the United States that approves new drugs.
The EMA (European Medicines Agency) is the regulatory authority for the European Union.
